PERSONNEL: James Horner- Composer - Star Trek II and III 

James was born on August 14th, 1953 in Los Angeles. At the age of 5 he began studying the piano and would go on to spend his formative years in London, where among other things, he would attend the Royal Academy of Music. He later earned a Bachelor's Degree in music from the university of Southern California after moving to the West Coast in the early 70's. At UCLA he went on to earn a Master's degree and a PhD in music composition and theory. 
It was while teaching music theory at UCLA in the 70's that Horner received a chance call from The American Film Institute and was ofered the chance to score their film, "The Drought." Horner instantly fell in love with scoring films and worked on several more AFI films before getting hired at Roger Corman's New World Pictures, where he would make a name for himself scoring sci-fi and horror films such as Lady in Red, Humanoids from the Deep and Battle Beyond the Stars. These projects brought him attention from the larger studios and after being hired to score Star Trek II: The Wrath of Khan, his career went into full gear. 
Since then he has worked on over 90 films including Star Trek III: the Search for Spock, Aliens, Willow, The Rocketeer, Patriot Games, Braveheart, Jumanji, Deep Impact, The Mask of Zorro, The Perfect Storm and Titanic, for which he won an Academy award, to name just a few. 
Horner currently resides in Southern California with his wife and two daughters.
