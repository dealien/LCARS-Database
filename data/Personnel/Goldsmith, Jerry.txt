PERSONNEL: Jerry Goldsmith - Composer 

Born: February 10, 1929 in Los Angeles, California, USA. 
Jerry Goldsmith started his career working as a clerk typist for CBS. After leaving this job, he started composing music for radio plays, and with the invention of television, he was writing for television shows by 1955. He wrote music shows such as "The Twilight Zone" in 1959, "Dr. Kildare" in 1961, and "The Man from U.N.C.L.E." in 1964. 
His first credited film score was written for the western "The Black Patch" in 1957. He was widely recognized for writing the music for the film "Lonely Are the Brave", starring Kirk Douglas. During that same year, he was nominated for his first Academy Award. 
Since then, Jerry has kept very busy writing film scores for such movies as "The Omen" (1973), "Star Trek: The Motion Picture (1979), "Rambo: First Blood Part II" (1985), "Star Trek V: The Final Frontier" (1989), "Star Trek: First Contact" (1996), and "Star Trek: Insurrection" (1998) He also wrote the main themes for "Star Trek: The Next Generation", and "Star Trek: Voyager". 
Jerry is married to his second wife Carol, and has 5 children.
