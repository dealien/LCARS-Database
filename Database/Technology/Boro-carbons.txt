CHEMISTRY: Boro-carbons

By product of reactions between tetrazine gas and plasma exhaust. Boro-carbons, also known as boron carbide, are obtained by treating graphite and boric acid in an electric furnace; hard brilliant blackish crystals. Used as abrasive, for boring rocks, in the manufacture of dies or electrodes.
